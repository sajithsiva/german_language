Sein — present tense & usage (German)

A concise, neatly formatted reference for the irregular verb sein (to be) and how sind is used.

Overview

In German, “sind” is a form of the verb sein (to be). It is the present tense, plural form used with wir, sie (they) and formal Sie (you).

sind → are

Wir sind müde. – We are tired.

Sie sind glücklich. – They are happy.

Sind Sie Herr Müller? – Are you Mr. Müller? (formal)

Quick tip

Sie (formal) always uses sind. Notice that sie (they) and formal Sie share the same verb form.

Key points

sind is used with wir, sie (they) and formal Sie.
ist is used for er/sie/es (he, she, it).
bist is the informal singular "you" (du).
Sein is irregular — you must memorize each form; it doesn’t follow regular endings.
Formal and plural overlap: Sie (formal you) takes the same verb form as sie (they), and wir also uses sind as well.

Why sein is irregular

Most German verbs follow regular patterns when conjugated: they keep the stem and add predictable endings. Sein does not — the whole word changes.

Regular verb example (machen — to do)

ich mache
du machst
er macht
wir machen
ihr macht
sie machen

The stem mach- stays the same.

Irregular verb example (sein — to be)

ich bin (not *seine)
du bist (not *seinst)
er ist (not *seint)
wir sind
ihr seid
sie sind

The whole word changes unpredictably — memorize each form.

Summary

“sind” = plural present of sein. Use it for wir, sie (they), and formal Sie. Because sein is irregular, learn the forms by heart.
